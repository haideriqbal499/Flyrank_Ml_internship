Keep it to **four pages**. Every box has one job against the claim and the one action.

```text

│  HOME                       │
│  Claim + one proof line     │
│  Primary CTA: Email me      │

               ▼

               │ who is this?
               ▼
┌─────────────────────────────┐
│  ABOUT                      │
│  Two sentences, same claim  │
└──────────────┬──────────────┘
               │ ready
               ▼
┌─────────────────────────────┐
```

| Page | Earns its place because… |
|---|---|
| Home | States the claim in one breath and puts the one action in reach. |
| Work (refresh queue) | The proof: ranked triage on messy search data + what it cannot claim. |
| About | Short human context so the hiring manager knows who emailed them. |
| Contact | The action page — no second ask, no newsletter, no “also hire me for X.” |

**Not on the core map (on purpose):** blog, services, résumé dump, “skills” page, multi-track case grid. Supporting links (repo, paper, framing case) can live *inside* Work or Home, not as equal nav destinations on week-1 sketch.

## Pressure-test prompt (run inside the Project)

```
This is my proof statement: I build ranked content-refresh queues on messy search data and say what they can and cannot claim. I am proving this to a hiring manager at an SEO, content, or applied-ML team who needs a useful prototype, not a flashy demo. The one action: email me to talk about an ML intern or junior role.

This is my sitemap:
1. Home — claim + proof line + Email me
2. Work — one case: ranked content-refresh queue (numbers + limits)
3. About — two-sentence bio
4. Contact — email only

Does this sitemap walk my one person from landing, to believing me, to taking my one action, and does it actually prove my claim? Tell me which pages earn their place, which are wasted, and what is missing. Be specific, not encouraging.
```

### Saved pressure-test answer (tutor pass — use or replace with Claude’s)

**Pages that earn their place**
- **Home** — required: claim + path to proof + CTA in view.
- **Work (refresh queue)** — required: this is the only page that can *prove* “ranking on messy search data + honest limits.”
- **Contact** — required: makes the one action unmistakable if they skip mailto on Home.
- **About** — earns a thin place: hiring managers often want a human line before emailing a stranger. Keep it short so it does not compete with Work.

**Wasted if treated as equal top-level pages**
- A separate “paper” and “frame-before-model” and “skills” nav items. They dilute the path. Put paper/repo/framing *inside* the Work case as optional depth, not sibling destinations competing with Email me.
- A hero where **Read the paper** is the primary button and Email is secondary. That trains the visitor toward reading, not acting.

**Missing**
- Nothing structural. What is missing is discipline: one primary CTA (Email me) on Home and Work, and one proof page that carries the claim alone.

### One thing I will change

**Change:** On Home, make **Email me** the primary button; move the paper/case to a secondary “see the proof” link. On the week-1 sitemap sketch, drop Paper and Frame-before-model from the top-level path so the hiring manager’s walk is four boxes only.

*(Live site can keep those pages linked from Work — they just should not own equal weight in the core map or the hero.)*
